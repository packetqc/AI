KALI LINUX DISCOVERY PROCEDURES
================================
Standard Operating Procedures — Internal Security Assessment
Version 1.0

This document describes the procedures for performing local host enumeration
and network discovery on a Kali Linux system during an authorized assessment.
Each section maps to a runbook procedure with discrete executable steps.


1. LOCAL HOST ENUMERATION
=========================

1.1  System Identity
    Collect basic host identification data before network activities begin.

    Step 1: sys_hostname — hostname -f 2>/dev/null || hostname
    Step 2: sys_uname    — uname -a
    Step 3: sys_uptime   — uptime -p && who | wc -l | xargs -I{} echo "{} user(s) logged in"
    Step 4: sys_kernel   — uname -r && cat /proc/version 2>/dev/null | head -1

1.2  Network Interfaces
    Document all active interfaces, routes, and cached neighbours.

    Step 1: iface_show  — ip addr show
    Step 2: route_show  — ip route show; ip -6 route show 2>/dev/null
    Step 3: arp_cache   — arp -n 2>/dev/null || ip neigh show

1.3  Running Services and Processes
    Identify what is listening and consuming resources on the host.

    Step 1: open_ports  — ss -tlnp
    Step 2: top_procs   — ps aux --sort=-%cpu | head -15
    Step 3: active_svc  — systemctl list-units --state=active --type=service --no-pager | head -20

1.4  Installed Security Tools
    Verify the Kali toolset is present before beginning network operations.

    Step 1: nmap_ver    — nmap --version
    Step 2: tools_list  — dpkg -l 2>/dev/null | grep -iE "nmap|wireshark|metasploit|burpsuite|aircrack|sqlmap|hydra|john|gobuster|ffuf|nikto|hashcat" | awk '{print $2}' | sort


2. NETWORK DISCOVERY
====================

2.1  Host Discovery
    Identify live hosts on the target subnet (default: 192.168.1.0/24)
    before deeper scanning.

    Step 1: ping_sweep  — nmap -sn 192.168.1.0/24 -oG - 2>/dev/null | grep "Up" | awk '{print $2}'
    Step 2: arp_local   — arp-scan --localnet 2>/dev/null || echo "tip: apt install arp-scan"
    Step 3: alive_hosts — fping -ag 192.168.1.0/24 2>/dev/null || nmap -sn 192.168.1.0/24

2.2  Port Scanning
    Scan discovered hosts for open ports at increasing levels of thoroughness.

    Step 1: quick_scan  — nmap -T4 -F --open 192.168.1.0/24
    Step 2: full_scan   — nmap -p- --min-rate=1000 -T4 --open 192.168.1.0/24
    Step 3: udp_top     — nmap -sU --top-ports 20 -T4 192.168.1.0/24

2.3  Service Enumeration
    Fingerprint services and operating systems on identified open ports.

    Step 1: ver_detect  — nmap -sV --version-intensity 5 --open 192.168.1.0/24
    Step 2: script_scan — nmap -sC --open 192.168.1.0/24
    Step 3: os_detect   — nmap -O --osscan-guess 192.168.1.0/24 2>/dev/null
